01
Why This Matters For You

SEEK Is Not Enough On Its Own

You've already applied through SEEK, company websites, recruiters and networking events. That's the right instinct — but most SOC and analyst roles never make it to a job board, or get 150+ applicants once they do. Cold outreach puts you in front of the hiring manager before the queue forms.

Your real constraint

You're working shift hours with no fixed days, and have roughly 7–10 hours a week for the job search. That means every hour needs to go toward the highest-leverage activity — and direct outreach to the person who actually hires converts far better per hour than scrolling job boards.

Your real edge

You're not a graduate hoping for a first chance. You've run live SOC monitoring in IBM QRadar and delivered VAPT engagements. That's a genuine, differentiated background — cold outreach lets you say that directly to a decision-maker instead of hoping a keyword filter notices it.

The Blocker You Named
You told us your biggest blocker is "not enough local experience." Cold outreach is how you neutralise that — a direct message that leads with your CEH, your live SOC work and your full work rights removes the local-experience question before it's asked.

Your Weekly Targets

The Volume That Actually Moves The Needle

Outreach only works with consistent volume. These are the numbers to hit every week — they fit inside your 7–10 hour budget if you batch them.

15–20
Targeted applications per week — SEEK, LinkedIn Jobs and direct company sites combined
3–5
Direct LinkedIn connection requests to hiring managers or SOC leads for every application you submit
2
Cold emails per week to security leads at target companies, outside of any live job ad
How To Fit This Into Shift Work
Batch it around your Wednesdays and any recurring days off. One 90-minute block for applications and connection requests, one 30-minute block for follow-ups on messages already sent. Outreach doesn't need to happen daily — it needs to happen on a schedule you can actually keep.

Objection Handling

What To Say When They Push Back

These are the two objections you're most likely to hit. Have the response ready before the conversation happens.

"We're looking for someone with local experience."

"I understand — what I'd point to is that the tools and workflows are the same wherever they're run. I was working IBM QRadar, correlating alerts and escalating confirmed threats in a live SOC for over a year, and running VAPT engagements with Nessus and Burp Suite for another six months. The alert triage and vulnerability assessment process doesn't change by country — and I'm already living in Sydney, available now, with no visa barrier to starting."

"Do you need sponsorship?"

"No — I'm on a partner visa with full working rights in Australia, so there's no sponsorship required and no visa timeline to plan around. I can start as soon as you need me to."

08
Weekly Execution Checklist

One Repeatable Weekly Cycle

Run this every week — it's designed to fit inside 7–10 hours around shift work.

  • Apply to 15–20 relevant roles (SEEK, LinkedIn Jobs, company sites)
  • Send 3–5 LinkedIn connection requests per application to the hiring manager or SOC/security lead
  • Send 2 cold emails to security leads at companies with no live ad open (Section 04)
  • Send first-message follow-ups to anyone who accepted a connection 1–2 days ago (Section 05)
  • Follow up on any email or message with no reply after 5–7 days
  • Register with one new specialist recruiter this week if not already done (Section 06)
  • Log every contact — name, company, date, response — so nothing gets chased twice or forgotten
Expected Outcome
Run this cycle consistently and you're no longer waiting on SEEK's algorithm — you're in direct contact with the people who make hiring decisions, every single week. Combined with your LinkedIn rebuild, this is what turns "not enough local experience" from an objection into a non-issue.
